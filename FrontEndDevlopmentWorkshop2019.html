Front End Devlopment Workshop (2019)

Previous Next

A workshop on Front end development was organized by the Computer Society of India - SFIT on the 12th and 13th of October, 2019, which was taken by Ms. Jayashree Domala(CSI chairperson) and Mr. Manmohan Dogra(CSI joint technical head) . It was attended by 67 students in total. A report for the same is as follows:  
All the students gathered at the ARC centre at 10:30 A.M. Mr Mohan started the session by briefing the students about the basic pillars of Web designing. He started with teaching how to Bootstraps. All students who had their personal laptops were made to install this software and the rest used the computer in the lab which had the software already downloaded. Using Bootstraps and by implementing simple HTML CSS codes a landing page was developed. A lunch break was given from 01:30 P.M. – 02:30 P.M. Post lunch, whatever was taught from the start of the session was revised by the speakers. The session for Day 1 ended at 05:00 pm with students learning the basic usage of Bootstraps and HTML CSS.
Day 2 started at 10.00 A.M. and where Mr Mohan started with where he had stopped the day before. He started by introducing JavaScript to the students. Basic introduction of JS, as well as its usage, was taught within the time period. A lunch break was given from 01:30 P.M. – 02:30 P.M. Post lunch, whatever was introduced in the previous session was put to use. Font Awesome concept was implemented by the speakers since Font Awesome is a font and icon toolkit based on CSS. Font Awesome gives a wide application in the Web Development sector and hence the students were taught its concept and application. By the end of the session, students were guided on how to make a full-fledged website on their own by utilizing the concepts learned throughout the seminar. Mr Mohan was very active while teaching all the topics. He readily solved the doubts of the students and proceeded accordingly. That’s how our seminar ended well and students had a productive and nourishing experience in the workshop.
